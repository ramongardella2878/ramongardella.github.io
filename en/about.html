I’m Ramón Gardella

I am a person who chose to dedicate his life to music. To do so, I left my home country, Argentina, and moved to Germany to continue my postgraduate studies in percussion. A few months after arriving in Cologne (the city where I have lived since 2015), I went through a deep crisis that prevented me from stepping onto the stage and playing. Or rather, every time I did step on stage, I felt that everything I was doing was wrong, that I was a failure, and that this path was not for me.

From that moment on, a process began in my life that has led me, ten years later, to become a successful musician with a growing career, collaborating and performing with important ensembles and orchestras throughout Europe, and developing artistic collaborations with countries across Latin America.

This process — which included therapy, courses, workshops, meetings, setbacks, and more doubts than certainties — was not only musical, but deeply personal. It was a process that made me realize I could not continue developing my music without paying attention to what I was feeling and to what was happening inside me.

I am where I am today because I chose to listen to what was happening within me and to work consciously on it.

Did I really have to go that far to realize this? Was it necessary to travel 14,000 kilometers and leave everything behind to start over in a foreign and cold country? Yes.

Looking back now, I can say it was the best — and the most difficult — decision I have ever made.

Now that I have come to understand many aspects of this process, I feel ready to support those who are going through something similar and/or resonate with this experience.

A successful musical career is not built solely through study and technical mastery of an instrument, but through personal development alongside it.
