Mental Notes
a space to listen to the inner life of a musician

One-to-one and group meetings for musicians who wish to deepen their personal process and, through that, develop a healthy musical life.

Intended for music students, graduates, professionals, amateurs, and anyone involved in artistic creation.

You Are in the Right Place

I designed this space to accompany you in your musical life, giving attention to what you feel when making music.

I start from the idea that the full development of a musical career is not possible if the performer does not also develop personally.

As musicians, we spend a large part — if not all — of our lives playing for others: competitions, lessons, auditions, entrance exams.

Experiences such as stage fright, frustration, constant comparison with colleagues, or the feeling of being stuck in one’s career exist — and they are not failures. They are a call to look inward and work on this personal dimension.

Is This for Me?

To know whether this is for you, you should meet the following criteria:

You are a musician, music student, music professional and/or artistic creator.

You feel that something in your artistic or musical life is blocked.

Something feels difficult and cannot be solved by improving physical skills alone.

You want to fully live your art and expand your career.

You identify with one (or several) of these statements:

"When I play, I feel uncomfortable"

"The difficult passages never work out"

"I practice all the time, but I know it won’t work in front of an audience"

"I can’t make a living from music"

"I feel stuck in my career"

"I look at what others do and feel I do so little"

"What will my parents / friends / partner think of me?"
